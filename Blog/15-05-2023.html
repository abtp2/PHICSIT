The ultimate guide to upskilling for an AI-integrated future

Sanjay Vivekanandan

---- Author

Date :15-05-2023

Read Time :6 min

To begin with, some of you might be thinking that you already know what this blog contains, but my sole purpose here is to open your minds to a whole new spectrum of upskilling. Read carefully to grasp and empower yourself.

INTRODUCTION :

As the world seems to rapidly evolve with the advent of artificial intelligence (AI), it’s becoming seemingly evident to stay ahead of the curve in terms of gaining skills and knowledge. Upskilling, which means nothing but the process of acquiring new skills or amplifying existing ones, is becoming, or rather, has already become, a critical component of staying afloat in today’s workforce arena.   AI continues to disrupt industries and change the nature of work, and it is set to displace nearly 85 million jobs worldwide by 2025 (YUP, YOU HEARD ME RIGHT). This is the perfect time and the perfect place to be in order to get ahead of our global neighbours.

THE REASON WHY YOU CAME :

Now Coming to our topic of "upskilling" in the context of an AI-integrated future, it simply means learning new skills that complement or enhance the work that machines can do rather than trying to compete with them. I want to make sure I get one thing straight through every thick cranial box: artificial intelligence is a human-made entity for the betterment of life. Keeping that in mind, humans can never be completely replaced by AI.   Soft skills such as creativity, emotional intelligence, and critical thinking are at the core of upskilling, as they cannot be automated with ease. Upskilling isn’t just beneficial for individuals; it’s also important for businesses and society as a whole. By equipping workers with the skills they need to succeed, we can reduce inequality and create a more equitable and prosperous society.   With all that being said, I'm sure that now there must be a ‘million dollar question’ rolling at the tip of your tongues: "What are the methods of upskilling and how do I gain access to them?" Do not worry, because I’ve got you covered.

YOUR 5-STEP SOLUTION :

So, here are 5 of the best proven methods for upskilling in an AI-integrated future, simplified for you: Identify the skills that are in demand:

  • 1) The number one key to success in any field imaginable This can be done by vigorously researching and staying up-to-date with emerging trends and technologies.
  • 2) Take advantage of employer-sponsored training programmes. Take advantage of these opportunities to learn new skills and gain valuable certifications that can enhance your career prospects.
  • 3) Pursue online learning opportunities: We live in the goddamn 21st century, so why not use online learning as it can be a flexible and cost-effective way to upskill, and many platforms offer self-paced courses that allow you to learn at your own pace?
  • 5) Practise and apply new skills: All comes to ruin without this step. The most effective way to upskill is to practise and apply your new skills in real-world settings. Consider taking on freelance or volunteer projects that allow you to gain practical experience and build your portfolio.

With these 5 methods, followed by the TOP 1%, you can develop the goodies needed to stay competitive in an AI-integrated future. However, it is important to remember that the process of upskilling is a lifelong one and needs to be constantly reviewed and followed with commitment.   I wouldn’t usually do this on any of my other blogs, but as this is a very important and urgent subject to be addressed, I’m sure a quick summary could be of some help.

Summary (for the lazy ones out there) :

Upskilling is an essential part of thriving in an AI-integrated future. By continuously learning new skills and improving existing ones, workers can stay competitive, relevant, and valuable in an increasingly automated world.

  • 1) Identify the skills that are in demand.
  • 2) Take advantage of employer-sponsored training programmes.
  • 3) Pursue online learning opportunities.
  • 4) Seek mentorship and guidance.
  • 5) Practise and apply new skills.

Different methods of upskilling may be more appropriate for different individuals, depending on their learning style, career goals, and personal circumstances.   Baseline: The most important thing is to take action and commit to continuous and relentless efforts.   I would like to conclude with a famous quote, "I am Still Learning," by Michelangelo at the age of 87.
